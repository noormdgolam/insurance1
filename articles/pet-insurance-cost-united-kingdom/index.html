How Much Is Pet Insurance in United Kingdom? A 2026 Guide

Pet insurance is a crucial investment for pet owners in the United Kingdom, providing financial protection against unexpected veterinary bills. However, the cost of pet insurance can vary significantly depending on several factors, including the type of pet, age, breed, and pre-existing medical conditions.

In this guide, we'll break down the average costs of pet insurance in the UK, outline the factors that affect the cost, and provide tips on how to save money on your pet insurance premiums.

Average Costs of Pet Insurance in the UK

  • Dog insurance:
    • Basic policies (accident-only): £10-£25 per month
    • Mid-range policies (accident and illness): £25-£50 per month
    • Comprehensive policies (lifetime cover): £50-£100 per month
  • Cat insurance:
    • Basic policies (accident-only): £5-£15 per month
    • Mid-range policies (accident and illness): £15-£30 per month
    • Comprehensive policies (lifetime cover): £30-£60 per month
  • Other pets (e.g., rabbits, guinea pigs): £10-£30 per month

Factors That Affects Cost

  • Pet's age and health:
    • Older pets or those with pre-existing medical conditions may attract higher premiums.
  • Pet's breed:
    • Purebred or high-risk breeds may be more expensive to insure.
  • Owner's age and health:
    • Younger owners or those with lower life expectancy may face lower premiums.
  • Policy excess and deductible:
    • Policies with lower excess and deductible may costs more upfront.
  • Level of cover and benefits:
    • Policies offering additional benefits (e.g., dental, optical) or higher coverage limits may be more expensive.

Tips for Saving on Pet Insurance

  • Compare policies and premiums from multiple insurers to find the best deal.
  • Consider a lower excess or deductible to reduce premiums.
  • Limit coverage to essential benefits, such as accident and illness.
  • Look for policies that offer a multi-pet discount.
  • Shop around for new policies if your pet's circumstances change (e.g., age, health).
  • Consider a temporary or seasonal policy for pets that are less likely to require ongoing care (e.g., outdoor cats).

When selecting a pet insurance policy, it's essential to balance cost with the level of cover and benefits. Consider your pet's individual needs, lifestyle, and health requirements to ensure you find a policy that provides the right level of protection without breaking the bank.
