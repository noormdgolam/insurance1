Chihuahua Insurance Cost: What to Expect in 2026

By Editorial Team | Last updated: July 11, 2026

The Chihuahua is one of the most popular breeds of dog in the world, known for its small size and large personality. As with any pet, owning a Chihuahua comes with a range of responsibilities, including providing them with the proper care and attention they need to live a long and healthy life. This includes regular veterinary check-ups, vaccinations, and preventative care, such as heartworm medication and flea control. However, accidents can still happen, and that's where Chihuahua insurance comes in.

Average Monthly Premiums for Chihuahua Insurance

The average monthly premium for Chihuahua insurance can vary depending on a range of factors, including the age and health of the dog, the location where you live, and the level of coverage you choose. Deductibles are the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. The deductible for Chihuahua insurance can vary depending on the level of coverage you choose and the provider you work with. Here are some estimated deductibles for Chihuahua insurance:

Breed-Specific Hereditary Conditions in Chihuahuas

Like all breeds of dog, Chihuahuas are prone to certain health problems that can be costly to treat. Some of the most common breed-specific hereditary conditions in Chihuahuas include:

  • Hypoglycemia: a condition in which the dog's blood sugar levels drop too low, leading to seizures and even death.
  • Patellar luxation: a condition in which the kneecap slips out of place, causing pain and discomfort.
  • Tracheal collapse: a condition in which the windpipe collapses, making it difficult for the dog to breathe.
  • Heart problems: Chihuahuas are prone to a range of heart problems, including mitral valve disease and pulmonic stenosis.
  • Legg-Calve-Perthes disease: a condition in which the hip joint degenerates, leading to arthritis and mobility problems.
  • Progressive retinal atrophy: a condition in which the eyes degenerate, leading to blindness.

Coverage Options for Chihuahua Insurance

When it comes to Chihuahua insurance, there are a range of coverage options to choose from. Here are some of the most common coverage options:

  • Accident-only coverage: covers costs associated with accidents, such as broken bones or lacerations.
  • Illness coverage: covers costs associated with illnesses, such as hip dysplasia or heart problems.
  • Wellness coverage: covers costs associated with preventative care, such as vaccinations and heartworm medication.
  • Elective surgery coverage: covers costs associated with elective surgeries, such as spaying or neutering.
