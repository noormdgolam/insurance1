Shih Tzu Insurance Cost: What to Expect in 2026

As a popular companion dog breed, Shih Tzus require regular veterinary care, which can be costly. To ensure your Shih Tzu receives the best possible care, a pet insurance plan is essential. In this article, we'll explore the average insurance costs, factors that affect premiums, and provide valuable tips on how to save on your Shih Tzu's insurance.

Average Cost of Shih Tzu Insurance

The average cost of Shih Tzu insurance is around $50 to $75 per month. However, this can vary depending on factors such as age, location, and medical history. As Shih Tzus are prone to certain health issues, such as eye and respiratory problems, pet owners may need to pay more for coverage.

  • Mature Shih Tzus (5-9 years): $50-$65 per month
  • Senior Shih Tzus (10+ years): $65-$80 per month

Factors That Affect Insurance Cost

  • Age: Older dogs are more prone to health issues, making them more expensive to insure.
  • Location: Urban areas tend to have higher insurance premiums due to the increased cost of living.
  • Medical History: Dogs with pre-existing conditions or a history of costly medical procedures may require higher premiums or be denied coverage.
  • Breed: Certain breeds, including Shih Tzus, are prone to specific health issues, which can increase insurance costs.
  • Policy Limits: Higher policy limits result in higher premiums.

Insurance Providers and Prices

Popular Pet Insurance Providers for Shih Tzus in 2026

The following providers offer competitive plans for Shih Tzus:

  • Petplan: $45-$75 per month for mature Shih Tzus, $65-$90 per month for seniors
  • Trupanion: $35-$60 per month for mature Shih Tzus, $55-$80 per month for seniors
  • Nationwide Pet Insurance: $40-$70 per month for mature Shih Tzus, $60-$90 per month for seniors

Tips for Saving on Shih Tzu Insurance

To minimize your Shih Tzu's insurance costs, consider the following:

  • Get a quote from multiple providers: Compare prices and coverage options to find the best plan for your pet
  • Choose a higher deductible: A higher deductible can lower premiums, but be sure to budget for potential veterinary expenses
  • Lifetime coverage: Opt for a plan with lifetime coverage to ensure your Shih Tzu is protected for their entire life
  • Maintain your pet's health: Regular check-ups, vaccinations, and preventative care can help prevent costly medical issues
  • Consider a wellness plan: Some providers offer discounted wellness plans or riders to cover routine care

Ultimately, finding the right pet insurance plan for your Shih Tzu requires careful research and consideration of your pet's individual needs and circumstances. By comparing prices, selecting the right provider, and following our saving tips, you can find a plan that works within your budget and ensures your Shih Tzu receives the care they deserve.
