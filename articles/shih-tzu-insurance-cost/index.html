Shih Tzu Insurance Cost: What to Expect in 2026

By Editorial Team | Last updated: July 11, 2026

The Shih Tzu is a popular breed of dog known for its friendly, outgoing personality and distinctive long coat. However, like all breeds, the Shih Tzu is prone to certain health issues that can be costly to treat. In this article, we will discuss the average monthly premiums for Shih Tzu insurance, deductibles, breed-specific hereditary conditions, coverage options, and frequently asked questions (FAQs).

The average monthly premium for Shih Tzu insurance can vary depending on several factors, including the dog's age, health, and location. However, based on industry data, here are some estimated average monthly premiums for Shih Tzu insurance:

Breed-Specific Hereditary Conditions in Shih Tzus

Shih Tzus are prone to several breed-specific hereditary conditions, including:

  • Orthopedic problems: Hip dysplasia, patellar luxation, and cruciate ligament injuries are common in Shih Tzus.
  • Dental issues: Shih Tzus are prone to tooth decay, gum disease, and tooth loss due to their small jaw size and crowded teeth.
  • Eye problems: Shih Tzus are prone to eye issues such as proptosis (bulging eyes), entropion (eyelid problems), and cataracts.
  • Respiratory issues: Shih Tzus are prone to breathing difficulties due to their flat face and narrow airway.
  • Patellar luxation: This condition occurs when the kneecap slips out of place, causing pain and discomfort.
  • Cruciate ligament injuries: These injuries can occur due to trauma or degenerative joint disease.
  • Tracheal collapse: This condition occurs when the airway collapses, causing breathing difficulties.
  • Thyroid problems: Shih Tzus are prone to hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones.

Coverage Options for Shih Tzu Insurance

Shih Tzu insurance policies can vary depending on the provider and policy. However, here are some common coverage options:

  • Accident-only coverage: This type of coverage pays for veterinary expenses related to accidents, such as injuries or trauma.
  • Illness coverage: This type of coverage pays for veterinary expenses related to illnesses, such as infections or diseases.
  • Wellness coverage: This type of coverage pays for routine care, such as vaccinations, dental cleanings, and physical examinations.
  • Genetic coverage: This type of coverage pays for veterinary expenses related to genetic disorders, such as hip dysplasia or patellar luxation.
  • Elective coverage: This type of coverage pays for elective procedures, such as spaying or neutering.
